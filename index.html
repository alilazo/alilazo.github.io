Operating Principles

The rules behind how I design, ship, and maintain software.

01

Build For Operators

Great software respects the people using it under pressure. Interfaces should reduce steps, surface the right data, and make the next action obvious.

02

Automate The Boring

Legacy workflows often hide the highest leverage. I look for repeated manual steps, brittle handoffs, and spreadsheet work that can become reliable systems.

03

Use AI With Judgment

AI is a force multiplier, not a substitute for ownership. I use it to move faster while still reviewing architecture, edge cases, and long-term maintainability.

04

Keep The System Understandable

Readable code, clear data flows, and simple interfaces are operational advantages. Complexity is only worth it when it pays rent.
